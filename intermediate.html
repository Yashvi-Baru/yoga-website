Yoga for Intermediate

1.Utkatasana (Awkward Chair Pose)

Awkward Chair Pose is a standing pose that strengthens your lower body and gives a stretch to your upper back. It is part of Surya Namaskar and is often a transitional pose or starting position for other poses.

How to do -

  • Bend your knees until the thighs are almost parallel to the floor.
  • Keep your knees bent and your butt low as you lift your arms up.
  • To release, inhale and straighten your legs, lifting your body up through your arms.

Benefits -

  • Engages the muscles in your buttocks, hips, and thighs
  • Engages your core to stabilize you in the pose and works on developing your balance.

2. Dhanurasana (Bow Pose)

Dhanurasana or Bow pose is a backbending asana in hatha yoga and modern yoga as exercise. It is useful for Improves hip flexors, Stimulates digestion ,Strengthens your upper back ,Improves posture , Strengthens spine and hamstrings

How to do -

  • Begin the bow pose by lying on your stomach with your arms at your side and your palms facing up.
  • Slightly raise your upper body off the floor , Bend your knees and press your feet together, Use both hands to reach back and grab the outside of your ankles.
  • lift your heels while also lifting your thighs away from the floor, which should raise your head and upper torso..

Benefits -

  • Stretches the entire front of the body, ankles, thighs and groins, abdomen and chest, and throat, and deep hip flexors
  • Improves posture
  • Stimulates the organs of the abdomen and neck
  • Relieves Constipation, respiratory ailments, mild backache, fatigue, anxiety, and menstrual discomfort

3.Paripurna Navasana(Boat Pose)

An ab and deep hip flexor strengthener, Boat Pose, or Paripurna Navasana in Sanskrit, requires you to balance on the tripod of your sitting bones and tailbone to build mental and physical focus, inspiring a full-body awareness.

How to do -

  • Sit on the floor with your legs straight in front of you.
  • Exhale and bend your knees, then lift your feet off the floor, so that the thighs are angled about 45-50 degrees relative to the floor.
  • Stretch your arms alongside the legs, parallel to each other and the floor.

Benefits -

  • Strengthens the abdomen, hip flexors, and spine
  • Stimulates the kidneys, thyroid and prostate glands, and intestines
  • Helps relieve stress
  • Improves digestion

4 .Ustrasana (Camel Pose)

Bump up your energy by bending back into Camel Pose, called Urstrasana in Sanskrit. Urstrasana counteracts slouching and relieves lower back pain with a generous, heart-opening stretch while also helping to build confidence.

How to do -

  • Stand on your shins with your knees and feet hip-width apart.
  • Wrap your hands around your hips encouraging your tailbone to drop down while your lower belly lifts up to neutralize your pelvis.
  • Keep your hands on your hips and lift your heart up powerfully as you roll your shoulder heads back.
  • Hold here with hands on the hips for about 8 breaths.

Benefits -

  • Can help build confidence and empowerment
  • Strengthens your back muscles, back of your thighs, and buttocks
  • Improves posture and counteracts the effects of prolonged sitting and doing computer work
  • May help relieve back pain
  • Stretches your abdomen, chest, shoulders, front of your hips, and front of your thighs

5. Bakasana (Crane Pose)

A compact arm balance, Crane Pose/Crow Pose, called Bakasana in Sanskrit, encourages toning in the abs and the arms, strengthening in the core, and improves focus in the mind.

How to do -

  • Bend your knees slightly , Plant your palms firmly on the mat about a foot in front of your feet.
  • Bend your elbows straight back ,Place your knees on the backs of your upper arms.
  • Begin to bring your weight forward into your hands, lifting your head as you go.

Benefits -

  • Strengthens arms and wrists
  • Stretches the upper back
  • Opens the groins
  • Strengthens the abdominal muscles
  • Tones the abdominal organs

6 .Garudasana(Eagle Pose)

Bump up your energy by bending back into Camel Pose, called Urstrasana in Sanskrit. Urstrasana counteracts slouching and relieves lower back pain with a generous, heart-opening stretch while also helping to build confidence.

How to do -

  • Stand in Tadasana. Bend your knees slightly, lift your left foot up and, balancing on your right foot, cross your left thigh over the right.
  • Stretch your arms straight forward, parallel to the floor
  • Cross the arms in front of your torso so that the right arm is above the left, then bend your elbows.
  • Press the right hand to the right and the left hand to the left, so that the palms are now facing each other. .

Benefits -

  • Strengthens and stretches the ankles and calves
  • Stretches the thighs, hips, shoulders, and upper back
  • Improves concentration
  • Improves sense of balance

8 .Hanumanasana (Monkey Pose)

How to do -

  • Kneel on the floor ,Step your right foot forward about a foot in front of your left knee .
  • Rotate your right thigh outwardly ,lifting the inner sole away from the floor
  • Slowly slide your left knee back, straightening the knee and at the same time descending the right thigh toward the floor.

Benefits -

  • Stretches the thighs, hamstrings, groins
  • Stimulates the abdominal organs

9.Anantasana (Side-Reclining Leg Lift)

How to do -

  • Lie on the floor on your right
  • Stretch your right arm straight out along the floor parallel to your torso
  • Bend your right elbow and support your head in your palm
  • Rotate your left leg so the toes point toward the ceiling, then bend and draw the knee toward your torso.
  • take hold of the left big toe with your index and middle fingers.

Benefits -

  • Stretches the backs of the legs
  • Stretches the sides of the torso
  • Tones the belly

10 .Bhujapidasana (Shoulder-Pressing Pose)

This arm balance relies more on precise positioning than on strength, making it more accessible for students beginning an arm balancing practice.

How to do -

  • Squat with your feet a little less than shoulder width apart, knees wide.
  • keeping your torso low, raise your hips until your thighs become close to parallel to the floor.
  • Snug your upper left arm and shoulder under the back of your left thigh just above the knee, and place your left hand on the floor , Then repeat on the right
  • Press your inner hands firmly against the floor and slowly begin to rock your weight back, off your feet and onto your hands.

Benefits -

  • Strengthens and stretches the ankles and calves
  • Stretches the thighs, hips, shoulders, and upper back
  • Improves concentration
  • Improves sense of balance
